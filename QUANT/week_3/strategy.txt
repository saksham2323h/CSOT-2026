Quant CSOT — Week 3 Challenge: Strategy Overview

---

## 1. Executive Summary
This document outlines a quantitative equity trading strategy designed for the Quant CSOT Week 3 Challenge. The strategy relies on an ensemble machine learning approach, combining a tree-based model and a linear model to predict cross-sectional forward returns. It utilizes a daily selection of long and short positions while strictly adhering to maximum weight and dollar-neutrality constraints. 

## 2. Data & Feature Engineering
The model is trained on a universe of 2,167 stocks over 5,058 trading days. 

* Input Features: The dataset consists of 22 technical indicators. 
* Top Feature Importance: The most predictive features identified during training include Ease of Movement, 60-day Volatility, 20-day Volatility, Ichimoku, and Average True Range.
* Target Variable: The models are trained to predict 5-day lagged, cross-sectionally ranked forward returns.
* Data Processing: Features are strictly cross-sectionally standardized (z-scored) on a daily basis to maintain mean 0 and standard deviation 1, with missing and infinite values replaced by 0.0. 
* Sampling: To optimize memory, the training dataset is sampled every 5 days, resulting in 750,000 training samples.

---

## 3. Predictive Modeling
The predictive engine utilizes a blended ensemble of two distinct machine learning models to balance non-linear interactions and linear stability. 

* Primary Model: A LightGBM Regressor configured with a learning rate of 0.03, max depth of 4, and 31 leaves. 
* Secondary Model: A Ridge Regression model with an alpha penalty of 100. 
* Ensemble Weighting: The final predictive alpha is calculated as a 60% weight to the LightGBM predictions and a 40% weight to the Ridge predictions. 
* Validation: An Out-Of-Sample (OOS) evaluation on the final 20% of the training timeline yielded a true OOS Spearman Information Coefficient (IC) of 0.0236.
* Final Training: The final submission model is trained on the entirely available dataset (no data leakage due to the strict out-of-sample test period constraint) using the optimal number of trees found during early stopping.

---

## 4. Signal Processing & Portfolio Construction
The daily `get_weights` function generates target allocations while enforcing strict capacity, position limit, and dollar-neutral constraints. 

* Signal Smoothing: To reduce turnover, the raw ensemble predictions are smoothed using an Exponential Moving Average (EMA) approach. 
* Smoothing Formula: The daily signal is blended as 70% of the current day's prediction and 30% of the previous day's signal. 
* Candidate Selection: The portfolio selects the top N stocks for the long leg and the bottom N stocks for the short leg. 
* Dynamic Tranching: The variable N is dynamically calculated as the maximum of 2 and the minimum of 10 or 20% of the daily tradable universe.
* Base Sizing: Initial weights are distributed equally on the smaller side to ensure gross capacity is never wasted, capped at an absolute maximum of 0.1 (10%) per stock. 
* Dollar-Neutral Scaling: The gross positive and absolute negative weights are calculated, and the larger side is scaled down to match the smaller target, enforcing standard dollar neutrality. 
* Residual Distribution: Any microscopic residual net weight is distributed evenly across all active, non-zero positions to prevent artificial weight spikes in any single asset.

## 5. Backtest Performance
An initial backtest run over the provided data period generated the following summary metrics:

* Turnover: 72.29%
* Gross Sharpe Ratio: -0.4135
* Net Sharpe Ratio: -0.4884
* Mean Book Value: 0.9960

Note: The strategy successfully passes all local validation checks, including unit capital limits, maximum individual weight caps, dollar neutrality bounds, and respect for the daily tradable universe.
